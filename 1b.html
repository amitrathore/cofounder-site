1B.

Unleash the power of one. A field note on the AI-empowered future of solopreneurship.

We are entering an era in which one person, paired with a fluent stack of agents, can credibly chase a billion-person, trillion-dollar market. Not as fantasy — as a Tuesday.

Rocketships of value.

For most of modern history, scale required a building, a payroll, and a board. Distribution was a privilege of the well-capitalized. Production was a privilege of the well-staffed. The cost of trying something new was measured in years and headcount, which is why almost no one tried.

AI dissolves that cost. Research, design, code, copy, ops, support, even go-to-market — the work that used to demand twenty people can now be carried by one human and a quiet swarm of agents. The bottleneck is no longer capacity. It is taste, judgment, and nerve.

What this unlocks is not a smaller business. It is a different shape of business altogether: small in headcount, planet-scale in reach. Rocketships of value piloted by one.

The one-billion frame.

The frame is deliberately audacious. A billion people. A trillion dollars of latent demand. The point isn't to capture all of it — it is to refuse the lazy ceilings that founders have been trained to accept. Alternative health. Wine. Travel. Music. Women entrepreneurs. Small geographies with outsized cultural gravity. Each one is already a market the size of a country.

Once you size opportunity at planet scale, your strategy changes. You stop optimizing for a niche and start composing for a civilization. The agents come along for the ride.

The unit of ambition is no longer the team. It is the individual, fully amplified.

Effective humanism.

The risk of an AI-saturated economy is obvious: software eats people, attention gets strip-mined, and the soul of the work goes missing. Effective humanism is the counter-discipline. Use the machines to do more of what is uniquely human — care, curiosity, craft, contact — not less.

A solopreneur operating from this stance treats agents as instruments, not replacements. The output is leveraged. The intent stays human. The customer meets a person, not a funnel.

Unicorn riders.

This future does not belong to everyone. It belongs to the people willing to ride animals that mostly don't exist yet — the daring, the patient, the slightly unreasonable. Courage to pick a market that sounds too big. Resilience to keep going when the agents are stupid and the customers are quiet. Discipline to stay small on purpose.

If that sounds like you, you already know. The work is to begin.
